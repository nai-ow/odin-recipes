Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies are the iconic dessert everyone loves — crisp around the edges, soft and gooey in the center, with melted chocolate in every bite. They’re easy to make, super nostalgic, and perfect with a glass of milk (or eaten straight off the tray while still warm — no judgment).

Ingredients

Dry ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Wet ingredients:

  • 1 cup (2 sticks) unsalted butter, softened
  • 3/4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs

Add-ins:

  • 2 cups semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper or lightly grease them.
  2. Mix dry ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. Cream the butter and sugars: In a large bowl, beat the softened butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar together until light and fluffy.
  4. Add eggs and vanilla: Beat in the eggs one at a time, then add the vanilla extract.
  5. Combine wet and dry: Gradually mix the dry ingredients into the wet mixture until fully incorporated.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips (and nuts if using) with a spatula or spoon.
  7. Scoop the dough: Drop spoonfuls (about 1–2 tablespoons per cookie) onto the prepared baking sheets, leaving some space between each.
  8. Bake for 9–11 minutes or until the edges are golden but the centers are still soft.
  9. Cool the cookies on the baking sheet for 2 minutes, then transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely.
  10. Eat while warm, or store in an airtight container for up to a week (but let’s be real, they won’t last that long).
