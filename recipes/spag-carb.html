Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is a rich and creamy Italian pasta dish made with eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, pancetta, and freshly ground black pepper. It’s a quick meal that’s comforting, savory, and comes together in under 30 minutes with just a handful of ingredients. Traditional carbonara doesn’t use cream — the silky sauce is made by combining hot pasta with the egg and cheese mixture.

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 150g (5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano (or Parmesan)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste
  • 1 clove garlic (optional, for flavoring oil)
  • Olive oil (small drizzle)

Instructions

  1. Boil pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until al dente, reserving 1 cup of the pasta water before draining.
  2. Cook pancetta: In a large skillet over medium heat, add a drizzle of olive oil and cook the pancetta until it’s crispy and golden. If using, you can lightly sauté a clove of garlic in the oil for flavor, then discard it.
  3. Make sauce: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, yolk, and grated Pecorino Romano. Add lots of freshly ground black pepper.
  4. Combine: Add the drained spaghetti to the pan with the pancetta and remove from heat. Quickly pour the egg-cheese mixture over the pasta and toss vigorously to coat, adding splashes of the reserved pasta water to create a creamy sauce.
  5. Serve: Top with more Pecorino and black pepper. Serve immediately.
